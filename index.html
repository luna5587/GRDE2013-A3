Clean Up

Every year on the first Sunday of march, Australians unite to conserve the environment and end waste. Clean Up Australia Day challenges all Aussies to pop on some gloves, grab a rubbish bag and come together to clean up their community.

What's the big issue?

One of Australia’s biggest crises - waste.
Australians are creating more waste than ever, so we need to reduce the rubbish that enters our environment and the waste that goes to landfill. With rampant consumerism, unnecessary waste and poor recycling habits, Australians are making too much rubbish - and not doing enough about it.

What can I do?

You can help clean up Australia with these easy activities.
Even small things like these make a big difference.

Download our free app

Recycle Mate is a game-changer as it removes the recycling guesswork, no matter where you are in Australia!

Learn about your council

Most councils have a website which will tell you what is accepted, and many bins list the materials that can be put in them.

Cash in your cans

Container deposit schemes provide a financial incentive to reduce litter, and meaning fewer recyclable materials are sent to landfill.

Battery Recycling

Australia has an official battery recycling scheme. Which means it's super easy to give your dead batteries new life.

Recycle Aluminium Foil

Scrunch up your aluminium foil into the size of a small fist, then place it in the yellow recycling bin.
